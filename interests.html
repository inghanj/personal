7.7.24: Few days ago when I was on holiday I finished reading the Conquest of Bread by Kropotkin. I thought it was pretty interesting and a good introduction. It did address a lot of common things about anarchy and communism, and it did spend quite a bit of time explaining the logistics of how a society would work, eg. how many people would be needed to work for how many hours to produce how much food, etc. He proposed that everybody does 5 hours of manual labour, the rest of the day would be on whatever else. 9.7.24: Didn't quite finish my thoughts here. So I was thinking, I think 5 hours of manual labor in good conditions, and with good company, would be alright. If it was with absolute shitheads, then nah, that wouldn't be alright. I've enjoyed doing manual labor but obviously not everyone enjoys it and not everybody can do it. The guy also made a point of how you can't really measure how much work is worth, and you can't calculate it in terms of hours either. I don't know yet what I think about that, whether we should have markets or not, whether we should have currency.
