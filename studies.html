For those not in Australia/victoria

ENGLISH LANGUAGE is mostly about linguistics and analysing speech. The most important stuff we learn in units 1/2 are about analysing texts (which can really be anything), using FIRTAC (certain aspects of texts such as function, intent, tenor...), a skill that is carried over to 3/4. We also learn a bit of child language acquisition (unit 1) and english through time (unit 2), which are pretty cool to know though it wouldn't exactly hurt you not to know them for unit 3/4.

MATH METHODS is the "second hardest" of the maths (foundation, general, methods, spesh). The content from unit 1/2 is quite similar to that of 3/4, in my experience it's just more in depth in 3/4 and it certainly is a bit trickier. For anyone doing unit 1/2 now or is planning to, I would advise you get a good grip on the concepts in 1/2 and really understand them fully, that will make 3/4 much smoother because it's a lot of the same stuff.

SPECIALIST MATHS is the "hardest" of the maths (though some argue methods is harder). So far in unit 1 we've looked at logic and proofs which are really cool to me (coming from seeing the same symbols in analytical philosophy), as well as algorithms, combinatorics and graph theory.

PHYSICS is pretty self explanatory. We've done light, heat, nuclear energy and electricity, and we just got started on motion.

MODERN HISTORY is also pretty self explanatory - we've learnt some about ideologies and the great depression. Next term we'll be doing the Cold War.

ART MAKING AND EXHIBITING is mostly about how art is curated and displayed in galleries, there's also a component of making your own art, and there's quite a lot of folio work. I haven't actually been to a class yet though.
